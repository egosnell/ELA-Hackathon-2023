THE PROBLEM ACAP FACES

A community is strongest when connected through a common goal. For the city of St. John New Brunswick, everyday life is centred around the water. From the industry it creates to the recreation it provides, it courses through the heart of the city. This presents a valuable opportunity to bring citizens into ACAP St. John’s monitoring and empower them with the information they need to become stakeholders in their environments. This is why our team has put together an interactive data visualiser accessible to every community member, or anyone else looking to access the invaluable decades of research collected. .

Our graphing tool will allow users to isolate and combine variables from these extensive datasets and display them in a line graph for easy visualizations of trends. Data can be selected by monitoring site or across all regional data through an interactive watershed map feature. Guideline values from the government of Canada will be displayed for each graph so users can see where results fall contextually.

We also aim to connect stakeholders from the area and across the country with a live community forum. This feature will allow citizens to report observations and ask questions they’ve had in a direct line to the organization monitoring their region.
